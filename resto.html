digiCROUS

INTRO

CROUS is an organization that provides meals for students. But there are some information barriers between students and CROUS. DigiCROUS is a mobile app to improve the dinning experience for students by making information accessible.

This is the project of the course Advanced Design of Interaction System.

TIME

Feb 2019



ROLE

Interview
Brainstorming
Prototype
Wireframe

The problem

The dining experience is not good. Students can't know today's menu in advance. The ingredients of the meal is not clear. And they wait too long before getting meals in peak hours.

We interviewed eight students and made a questionnaire which received 24 responses. Here are some results from the questionnaire.

The goal

Provide necessary information to students and help students track what they eat

    Question

    What kind of information do the students need?

    1. Information about food

    We wanted to display basic information about food. I designed a participatory design activity to get more ideas.

    Activity:

    1. Users will see some pictures of food and link whatever they think about to the food to the pictures.
    2. Users will try to group some food by color according to their feelings.

    Insight

    Students care about ingredients, nutrition and flavor. Using color to group food is confusing since they have different understanding about colors.

    2. Information about restaurant

    Students need the busyness and peak hour of each restaurant for better decision. And, they need a recharge notification if there is no enough balance in student card.

    We made some paper prototypes to test our ideas.

    Indicator for peak hour

    We designed an indicator to show the current status and peak hour of each restaurant.

    The tray in both real and digital world

    We designed a tray, like the real tray in CROUS. It indicated your choice. At CROUS, students can choose one plat, one dessert, one entry and one salad. Food will be added to the correspoding place on the tray.

    They can click the dish to see the details including name, nutritions, ingredients, allergic information and some comments.

    Thinking

    Reification

    This design can help users understand how to use the app and new student to understand CROUS rule.

    The filter

    We also designed a filter to help students who have diet restrications. For example, Ming could filter gluten out.

    Evaluation

    Filter or profile?

    Ming is allergic to gluten. But he has to set the filter everytime. He needs a permanent filter. So we designed a user profile. Users can set it at the first time using this app.

    The filter we originally designed, was for temporary use. For example, it can be used to choose different flavours.

    Design

    Fast way to access history

    Compared to other apps process, this design is really fast to help students in hurry.

    For fast choice, there was a problem when a specific food was not offered today or sold out. So we added a notification if it happeded. Students could add new food quickly in the popup.

    Design

    Track your food

    We added a nutrition indicator when choosing food. After eating, this app will send a report about nutrition and users can rate the food at this time.

    Insight

    Students want to eat with friends

    Users can share their locations to friends and eat with friends to make the lunch time more interesting.

    Thinking

    Fewer steps

    Normally, users should remove their location manually after finishing eating. But it's very easy to forget it and make their friends upset. We designed a timer to do this automatically.
