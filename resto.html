digiCrous

INTRO

Crous is a French organization that provides meals for students. But there are some information barriers between students and Crous. DigiCrous is a mobile app to improve the dinning experience by making information accessible.

This is the project of Advanced Design of Interaction System.

TIME

Feb 2019



ROLE

Interview
Brainstorming
Prototype
Wireframe

User research

We interviewed eight users using critical incident interview and made a questionnaire which received 24 responses.

Here are some results from the questionnaire.

From the interview and questionnaire results, we found that there were 4 common problems.

  • Waiting too long before getting meals
  • Students don't know the daily menu
  • The ingredients of the food is not clear
  • No balance reminder

    • Students could't get these information unless they went to the restaurant. So we decided to design a mobile app to provide necessary information to students and help students track what they ate.

    The other thing we found interesting was that students preferred to eat with friends. But sometimes it was hard to find friends in the crowded dinning hall.

    After brainstorming, we wanted to display basic information about food. I designed a participatory design activity to figure out what was basic.

    Activity: linking

    Users will see some pictures of food and link whatever they think about to the food and group some food by color according to their feelings.

    The result was similiar to what we expected. Users cared about ingredients, nutrition and flavor. But using color to group food was not good, since users had different understanding about colors.

    Prototyping

    When users clicked one of the restaurant, they could see today's food.

    When users clicked one of the food, they could see the details including name, nutritions, ingredients, allergic information and some comments.

    We also designed a filter function to help users who have diet restrications. For example, Ming could filter gluten out.

    Then we thought there should be two types of filter. One type of filter always applied for allergics, vegan, etc. The other filter, which we originally designed, was for temporary use.

    The first type of filter was like user settings. Users can set it at the first time using this app.

    Prototyping

    Users can see the busyness and peak hour of each restaurant for better decision.

    And, users can see a recharge notification if there is no enough balance in student card.

    We designed a fast way to access history orders.

    For fast choice, there was a problem when a specific food was not offered today or sold out.

    If it happeded, users would get a notification and they could add new food quickly.

    We added a nutrition indicator when choosing food.

    After eating, this app will send a report about nutrition and users can rate the food at this time.

    Users can share their locations to friends and eat with friends to make the lunch time more interesting.
