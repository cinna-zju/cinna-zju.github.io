cityller

INTRO

Cityller is a travel plan app based on an interactive map to help travellers make their itinerary quickly. The design idea is from paper maps to provide a immersional experience.

I worked with Yuanyang ZHONG, Lemeng XU and Qiwei GAO.

CLASS

2018 HCI bootcamp



ROLE

Interaction Designer

The problem

Travellers spend a lot of time making an itinerary before or during journey. They had to use different apps to make it. And travel plan app can't suggest some places based on location.

We interviewed 8 users, mainly university students, using story interview method.

Too complex

They searched interesting places, marked these places in Google maps and writed these information down in a text editor.

Not based on location

The map in the travel app is just for display and not interactable. So it's hard to do something based on current location.

If users want to make an itinerary after they arrived at the hotel, these app can't suggest some places near me.

The goal

Help travellers create their itinerary quickly and smoothly at any time.

We analysed the interview and find some issues behind the problem. Then we created 2 personas. Jon is a extreme personna who pushed the limits of the situation to test the usability.

Issue 1

They spend a lot of time jumping between different apps.

Issue 2

They don't know where to visit.

Addressing issue 1

Design a smooth experience for Jon

Insight

Creating an itinerary based on map is convenient and natural.

We compared the map apps, paper maps and travel plan apps. Users can get details from map apps and suggestions from travel apps. Our idea was to combine the experience of the three different tools.

Inspiration

The interaction with paper map

Before digital ages, travellers can browse and mark some interesting places on a paper map.

They can also draw lines to show the routes and visiting order.

We made a paper prototype to test the experience of drawing lines. Then I found some problems.

Problem 1

Drawing on a small screen is not comfortable

It is true that drawing app on the phone usually don't have good experience.

Problem 2

Lines are conflict with the actual transportation routes.

One object with two meanings is confusing.

Solution

Change marking to clicking.

Users can interact with the map directly. Clicking is a more common interaction with phone. And it's fast.

Solution

Use line to show the actual routes.

Nowadays, most travellers take public transportation and they don't know the routes. So we removed the drawing The routes will be calculated by this app and displayed as lines. The default order will be set as the order some places are added.

New Problem

How do users set order quickly without drawing?

We designed a sidebar, like a backpack, where users can set the visiting order or delete items by direct manipulation.

1. Delete
Users can simply swipe the bubble out from the sidebar.

2. Set visiting order
Users can drag and drop the place bubble to arrange the visiting order.

Users can view other's itinerary in both map view and itinerary view to get the relative location and possible transportations. And they can make some changes to meet their own needs.

Consistency

We designed a consistent experience for creating a itinerary and modifying other's itinerary. The interface and interaction were almost the same.

Problem

Users got on the metro to the wrong direction.

Some users told me they sometimes go to the wrong direction when using Google Maps. I analysed the transportation page of Google Maps and found some problems.

Redesign

Convey information step by step

Users can concentrate on what to do next and the important information are obvious.

Then I designed the details of card. Information are grouped and important information are highlighted.
