cityller

INTRO

Cityller is a travel itinerary app based on an interactive map, which provides an immersional experience with fewer page jumping.

This is originally a project of the one-week workshop 2018 HCI bootcamp, taught by Wendy Mackay at Université Paris Sud. I worked with Yuanyang ZHONG, Lemeng XU and Qiwei GAO.

User research

We interviewed eight users, mainly university students, using story interview method, by asking a user questions that elicit recent stories about making itinerary。

We analyzed the interviews, got some breakdowns, grouped these breakdowns and found the main three problems.

  • Making an itinerary on a map App is difficult
  • Watch their phones a lot of times to get insturctions when navigation
  • Itinerary apps don't provide transportation information

    • We created 3 personas to represent the users we designed for. Jon was the extreme personna who pushed the limits of the situation to test the usability.

    01 Creating itinerary smoothly

    This feature was designed for Jon to provide a smooth and quick experience of creating a itinerary.

    Brainstorming

    We compared the map apps, paper maps and travel plan apps. Then we thought bringing the drawing experience on paper map to the mobile device was a good idea because drawing was quickly and intuitively.

    Some basic interactions with paper map were marking something and drawing lines.

    We used instrumental design, brought up by Michel Beaudouin-Lafon in his paper, to generate ideas about how a user interacted with our app. The key principles are reification, polymorphism and reuse.

    Prototyping

    We made a paper prototype to simulate the interaction process and shot a video to capture interactions.

    Then I found some problems.

  • Drawing on small screen devices was not a comfortable interaction.
  • The drawing line would be conflict with the actual transportation routes

    • But we still wanted to keep immersional experience on paper map. I changed the drawing on map view to clicking and used the line to show order only. Then we designed a sidebar. The sidebar was the reification of a backpack.

    1. Delete
    Users can simply swipe the bubble out from the sidebar.

    2. Set visiting order
    Users can drag and drop the place bubble to arrange the visiting order.
