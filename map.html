cityller

INTRO

Cityller is a travel plan app, combining the advantages of different tools and provide an immersional experience.

I worked with Yuanyang ZHONG, Lemeng XU and Qiwei GAO.



CLASS

2018 HCI bootcamp



ROLE

Interaction Designer

The problem

Travellers spend a lot of time making a travel itinerary because they often jump between different apps

We interviewed 8 university students, using story interview method.

They didn't know where to visit. So they searched interesting places in Google. Then they marked these places in Google maps. And they writed some information, like opening hours and price, in a document. Then they do it agian to add more places and set a visiting order.



The goal

Help both experienced and novice travellers create their itinerary quickly and smoothly at any time.

Extreme Persona

I want to make an itinerary in seconds.

    • Brainstorming

    Bring the advantages of differnet tools together

    We compared the map apps, paper maps and travel plan apps. Users can get details from map apps and suggestions from travel apps. Our idea was to combine the experience of the three different tools.

    Insight

    Drawing on a paper map is natural

    Before digital ages, travellers can browse and mark some interesting places on a paper map. They can also draw lines to show the routes and visiting order.

    Problem

    Drawing on small screens is not comfortable

    We made a paper prototype and found drawing on a small screen was not comfortable, especially with fingers.



    Solution

    Change drawing to clicking.

    Users still can interact with the map directly. Clicking is a more common interaction with phone. But we lost some information, like visiting order, compared to drawing.

    New Problem

    How do users set order quickly without drawing?

    Idea

    Backpack sidebar

    From the way we use backpacks, we designed a sidebar, where users can set the visiting order or delete items by direct manipulation.

    1. Delete
    Users can simply swipe the bubble out from the sidebar.

    2. Set visiting order
    Users can drag and drop the place bubble to arrange the visiting order.

    Thinking

    Intuitive and quickly

    The interaction is simple and efficient. By using the idea of backpack, the sidebar is very close to user's mental model.

    Idea

    Edit in map, view in list

    The idea is to edit in map and get the result in itinerary view. Editing in map is more clear and natural. Users can see the location, relative distance and many other information. And in the itinerary view, they can glance the itinerary and find the transportation information.

    Users can view other's itinerary in both map view and itinerary. After choosing, they can edit it in map view.

    We used interaction table from instrumental design to generate ideas connecting the map and itinerary in our design.

    Natural Editing Experience

    Wireframe

    Create a new itinerary

    Thinking

    Show recommended places in different levels

    Users can zoom in and out to see different suggested places. At first, they will see the most famous attractions. Then if they don't find it, they can zoom in to see more but less popular places.

    Location based service is convenient

    When travellers didn't make a plan before their voyage starting, location based service can help them create a plan very quickly. For example, if travellers want to walk around after they arrived at the hotel, cityller can show the location of interesting places near the hotel.

    Wireframe

    Search a place

    Thinking

    Minimize distraction

    In the search page, users can see a search history and popular place. I designed a fast process for searching and adding, to minimize the distraction. After searching, users go back to editing page directly, only with the result highlighted. And they can click to see more information.

    Wireframe

    Choose other's itinerary and edit

    Thinking

    Consistency

    The interface and interaction are almost the same as editing own itinerary. Besides, this design can simplify the development. Many components can be reused.

    Insight

    Users got on the metro to the wrong direction.

    Some users told me they sometimes go to the wrong direction when using Google Maps. I analysed the transportation page of Google Maps and found some problems.

    Idea

    Convey information step by step

    Users can concentrate on what to do next and the important information are obvious. The goal of this design is to help users remember information about their next step. During each step, they don't need to watch their phone again.

    The idea came from the sticky notes. We used them one by one, put information of the same group on one page and arranged them freely.

    Then I designed the details of card. Information are grouped and important information are highlighted. The color of each cardis the same as the metro line.
